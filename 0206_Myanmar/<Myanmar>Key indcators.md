# Annual Report on Drug Control in Myanmar (2020-2024)

This document outlines the key drug-related statistical indicators consistently reported in the Annual Reports published by the **Central Committee for Drug Abuse Control (CCDAC)** of Myanmar. The data covers the period including 2020, 2022, 2023, and 2024.

**Data Source:** Central Committee for Drug Abuse Control (CCDAC), Myanmar

---

## 1. Supply Reduction (Law Enforcement)
Indicators related to the suppression of illicit drug trafficking, production, and cultivation.

### A. Enforcement Operations
* **Total Cases:** Number of drug-related cases registered.
* **Total Suspects (Offenders):** Total number of people arrested.
    * *Gender Breakdown:* Male / Female

### B. Illicit Drug Seizures
Quantities of major illicit drugs and precursors seized by authorities.

* **Opium**
* **Heroin**
* **Methamphetamine**
    * Tablets (WY)
    * Ice (Crystal Meth)
* **Cannabis (Marijuana)**
* **Ketamine**
* **Ecstasy (MDMA)**
* **Precursor Chemicals** (e.g., Caffeine, Ammonium Nitrate, Hydrochloric Acid, Sulphuric Acid, Sodium Cyanide)

### C. Eradication of Opium Poppy Cultivation
Efforts to destroy illicit opium poppy fields.

* **Total Area Eradicated:** Measured in Hectares (Ha).
* **Eradication by Location:** Breakdown by specific States/Regions (e.g., Shan, Kachin, Kayah, Chin).

---

## 2. Demand Reduction (Prevention & Treatment)
Strategies aimed at reducing the desire for and use of illicit drugs through education and rehabilitation.

### A. Preventive Education
Public awareness campaigns and educational activities.

* **Educational Talks/Lectures:**
    * Number of talks held.
    * Number of participants (Students, Parents, Locals).
* **Information Dissemination:** Distribution of pamphlets, journals, posters, and cartoons.
* **Public Engagement:**
    * Drug elimination museums and mobile exhibitions.
    * Student competitions (essays, poems, cartoons, arts, computer painting).

### B. Treatment and Rehabilitation
Statistics on infrastructure and patients receiving care.

* **Treatment Infrastructure:**
    * Number of Major Drug Treatment Centers (DTCs).
    * Number of Minor Drug Treatment Centers (DTCs).
    * Number of Methadone Maintenance Clinics (MMCs).
    * Number of Rehabilitation Centers.
* **Patient Statistics:**
    * Total number of drug users treated.
    * Breakdown by type of drug used (Opium, Heroin, Methamphetamine, Cannabis, etc.).
* **Harm Reduction:**
    * Number of patients receiving Methadone Maintenance Therapy (MMT).

---

## 3. International Cooperation
Engagement in global and regional frameworks to combat drug issues.

* **Bilateral Cooperation:** Meetings and activities with neighboring countries (China, India, Thailand, Lao PDR, Bangladesh) and Border Liaison Offices (BLO).
* **Regional & International Engagement:** Participation in ASEAN, Mekong MOU, BIMSTEC, and UNODC meetings.
* **Capacity Building:** Number of training sessions attended by officials (local and overseas).
